The web has existed for a long time, so it has had several versions that have considerably increased its efficiency:

1.0: data goes in one direction only: from the website to the user.

2.0: data goes both ways: internet users can interact with websites, leave comments, etc.

3.0: we don't talk about it... it's marketing. They call it the cloud, but everything is like before. It's also social networks, which aren't an innovation in themselves, but simply another way of presenting what already existed. So it's just a cloud of hot air.

4.0: first interactions with the real world and the senses: the addition of virtual reality, augmented reality and also sometimes holograms or 3D printers. All accelerated by the first innovation in input devices after the keyboard and mouse: eye tracking, the bidirectional interaction of sight; the understanding of eyes by computers.

5.0: Intersense. What BrainView is preparing: interaction with others, via the senses, before writing, before speech. Directly through thought. Publicized as the Ideal World.
