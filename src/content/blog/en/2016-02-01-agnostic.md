When we think about death, we often think of that tunnel with a white light at the end, as many accounts testify. 

This tunnel could be just a final hallucination of a brain lacking oxygen and stimuli. 

There may also be something else after death. The problem is that you have to die to know, and the dead don't talk much. 

The agnostic doesn't deny any religious or spiritual hypothesis, no matter how far-fetched it may seem. There's simply no proof to know what's true, so the agnostic abstains, giving equal credit to all possibilities, most of the time without practicing any ritual, any belief, any superstition. 

However, most agnostics would like to believe in an entity greater than us, this being one of the differences from atheists, who deny any divinity or higher entity.
