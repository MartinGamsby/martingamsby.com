Isaac Newton was centuries ahead of his time, defining concepts still massively used today. Yet he didn't have many friends, and was sometimes so absorbed by his work that he forgot to eat.

Otherwise obsessed with routines, he sometimes gave lectures to empty rooms, if no one had shown up, simply because the lecture was scheduled. For these reasons, among others, some think he was autistic.
